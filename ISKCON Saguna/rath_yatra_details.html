Location: Jagannath Temple, Puri

The Sacred Journey of Lord Jagannath

Rath Yatra, the Chariot Festival of Lord Jagannath, is one of the oldest and grandest Hindu festivals celebrated with immense devotion in Puri, Odisha. This spectacular procession commemorates the annual journey of Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra to their aunt's temple (Gundicha Temple).

The festival symbolizes the Lord's desire to come out of the temple to bless all devotees, regardless of caste, creed, or religion. The massive, elaborately decorated chariots are pulled by thousands of devotees along the Bada Danda (Grand Road) amid chanting of hymns and beating of traditional drums.

Sacred Route of the Chariots

The grand procession covers the 3km Bada Danda from Shree Mandira to Gundicha Temple, passing these sacred sites:

  • Singhadwara (Lion's Gate)
  • Balagandi (Starting point of chariot pulling)
  • Sahid Minar (Midway point)
  • Gundicha Temple (Final destination)
